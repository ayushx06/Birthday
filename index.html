A Letter For You

"

My dearest Prakriti,

There are some people who walk into your life quietly and rearrange everything — not with noise, but with warmth. You were that person for me. I still remember the first time I saw you at college, my junior, carrying yourself with that quiet confidence and a smile that could make the whole world pause. I didn't know then that you would become the most important person in my universe.

Across every kilometre that now separates us — from the hills of Nepal to the shores of New Zealand — I carry you with me. In every morning when the light is soft. In every evening when I miss you most. In every beautiful thing I see, I think: she would love this. You have a way of making the world feel more alive simply by existing in it.

You were born on Asar 12 — the monsoon month, when Nepal turns impossibly green and the rivers run full. It feels so right. That you came into this world when everything was blooming. Because that is what you do — you make things bloom. You make people around you feel more alive.

I love the way you carry your culture with grace. Your smile, your sarees, the mountains behind you in every photo as though Nepal itself is proud to be your backdrop. I love your gentleness and your strength. I love that you are both soft and utterly unshakeable.

This birthday, I cannot hold your hand or watch your face light up in person — and that distance is a quiet ache I carry every single day. But know this: every thought I send across the sky, every wish I make tonight, every day I work toward a future where the miles between us disappear — all of it is for you.

So on this day, from 7,000 kilometres away, with every piece of my heart — Happy Birthday, Prakriti. You deserve all the roses, all the light, all the joy this world holds. I love you endlessly, completely, and always.

— Yours, always 💕
